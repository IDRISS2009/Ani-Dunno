ONE PIECE

One Piece (Japanese: ワンピース Hepburn: Wan Pīsu) is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Eiichiro Oda. It has been serialized in Shueisha's Weekly Shōnen Jump magazine since July 22, 1997, and has been collected into 90 tankōbon volumes. The story follows the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy, a boy whose body gained the properties of rubber after unintentionally eating a Devil Fruit. With his crew of pirates, named the Straw Hat Pirates, Luffy explores the Grand Line in search of the world's ultimate treasure known as "One Piece" in order to become the next Pirate King.

The manga has been adapted into an original video animation (OVA) produced by Production I.G in 1998, and an anime series produced by Toei Animation, which began broadcasting in Japan in 1999. Additionally, Toei has developed thirteen animated feature films, one OVA and eleven television specials. Several companies have developed various types of merchandising such as a trading card game and numerous video games. The manga series was licensed for an English language release in North America and the United Kingdom by Viz Media and in Australia by Madman Entertainment. The anime series was licensed by 4Kids Entertainment for an English-language release in North America in 2004, before the license was dropped and subsequently acquired by Funimation in 2007.

One Piece has received praise for its storytelling, art, characterization, and humor. Several volumes of the manga have broken publishing records, including the highest initial print run of any book in Japan. The official website for Eiichiro Oda's One Piece manga announced that the manga has set a Guinness World Record for "the most copies published for the same comic book series by a single author". As of May 2018, the manga has sold over 440 million copies worldwide, making it the best-selling manga series in history. It became the best-selling manga for the tenth consecutive year in 2017.

DEVELOPMENT

When Eiichiro Oda created Luffy, he strove for a "manliness" similar to that of Akira Toriyama's Dragon Ball series. Oda said that he named his main character "Luffy" because he felt that the name suited him. When he later learned about the sailing term, "luffing", he was delighted by the coincidence. In his prototype one-shots, "Romance Dawn", Oda refined the artistic style and story elements before publishing the final product a year later as the first chapter of One Piece. In the second version of "Romance Dawn", Luffy resembled his design at the beginning of the series. To please his readers, Oda added rubberization to Luffy for comic effect and tries to make the character straightforward.

Episodes

One Piece is an anime series that has been ongoing for many years, featuring numerous episodes that follow the adventures of Monkey D. Luffy and his crew in their quest to find the ultimate treasure, the One Piece.

Season 1

Number of episodes: 61

Air Date: October 1999 - March 2001

Main Arcs:

  • East Blue Arc (Episodes 1-3): Introduction of Luffy and his dream of becoming the Pirate King.
  • Loguetown Arc (Episodes 45-48): The crew visits the town where Gol D. Roger was executed.
  • Arlong Park Arc (Episodes 31-44): Nami's backstory is revealed as the crew confronts Arlong.
  • Loguetown Arc (Episodes 45-48): The crew arrives in Loguetown, the place where Gol D. Roger was born and executed.
  • Sir Crocodile Arc (Episodes 49-61): The Straw Hats face the powerful Warlord Sir Crocodile.

Season 2

Number of episodes: 42

Air Date: March 2001 - March 2002

Main Arcs:

  • Alabasta Arc (Episodes 92-130): The crew helps Princess Vivi save her kingdom from Crocodile.
  • Post-Alabasta Arc (Episodes 131-135): The Straw Hats deal with the aftermath of the Alabasta arc.
  • Sky Island Arc (Episodes 174-195): Luffy and his crew explore the mysterious Sky Island.

Season 3

Number of episodes: 50

Air Date: March 2002 - March 2003

Main Arcs:

  • Sky Island Arc (Episodes 174-195): The Straw Hats face the challenges of the Sky Island and the powerful Enel.
  • Water 7 Arc (Episodes 207-228): The crew arrives in Water 7, where they confront the CP9 and the world government.
  • Enies Lobby Arc (Episodes 229-263): A major showdown at Enies Lobby to rescue Nico Robin.
